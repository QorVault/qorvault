## 1. FINANCIAL TIMELINE (What Was Known, When)

### Pre-2020: Historical Context
- **2005-2011**: District experienced cyclical budget crises driven by state funding cuts (Great Recession). In 2009, district implemented first RIF in a decade (129.28 positions). In 2010-11, five budget reduction scenarios were presented, with furlough days costing $759,822/day.
- **2017**: Superintendent Watts acknowledged "financially challenging times"; OSPI and PSESD were providing assistance.
- **2018 (October)**: Fiscal Recovery Task Force established with detailed action plan including monthly monitoring, dashboards, and co-presentations to the board. Budget revised due to 2018-19 enrollment decline of ~700 students vs 2017-18; revenue decreased $7.97 million (Resolution 1554).
- **2019**: 65.6 FTE certificated positions eliminated through attrition (no RIF) due to enrollment decline.

### 2020-2022: ESSER Masking the Structural Deficit
- **2020-21**: Budgeted 26,418 FTE; actual 25,323 -- variance of -1,095 students. Pandemic-era enrollment collapse begins.
- **2021-22**: Budgeted 25,776 FTE; actual ~24,683-24,697 -- variance of -1,079 to -1,093 students.
- **June 2022**: Budget work sessions EXPLICITLY identified "structural deficit" -- defined as fundamental imbalance between receipts and expenditures. Presentation noted district would "Spend Roughly $30 million/year in Non-Recurring Revenue leading up to 2024" and asked "How does this Revenue Get Replaced?"
- **August 2022**: FY23 budget included "2024 Planning" for "Off-Ramping from ESSER in 2024-25 SY" and "Keeping Close Track of Structural Deficit."

### 2023: Year-End Warning Signs
- **November 2023**: FY23 Year-End Presentation showed 10-year enrollment history, the ESSER cliff, and a structural deficit of $31.5 million.

### 2024: Deficit Becomes Acute
- **January 24, 2024**: Financial status update identified key metrics board should track. Superintendent Vela stated he watched the fund balance "continue to dwindle" from his second day. He said "no action is not the right direction" and framed the issue as requiring sustained multi-year action for "the future of fiscal stability." Board member noted district "was X'd out of the box of levy equalization funds costing us $7 million a year."
- **March 13, 2024**: Budget Work Session #1 adopted "multi year stair-step budget strategy" with reductions focused first on central office.
- **June 26, 2024**: Budget Work Session #3 presented four-year General Fund forecast showing deficits in EVERY projected year:
  - FY2024-25: -$9.65 million
  - FY2025-26: -$6.50 million
  - FY2026-27: -$7.24 million
  - FY2027-28: -$12.83 million
- **July 10, 2024**: Public Hearing showed same deficit projections with slightly higher beginning balance ($55.87M vs $55M -- $874K discrepancy unexplained).
- **August 21, 2024**: FY2024-25 budget adopted. Budget Director Tylor stated ending fund balance "around $46 million." **KEY DISCREPANCY**: Actual FY2023-24 ending fund balance was $69.79 million -- approximately $18.8 million more than the budgeted ~$51 million. This variance "was not disclosed."
- **December 4, 2024**: SAO audit exit conference for FY2023 (Sep 2022-Aug 2023): Unmodified opinion, no material weaknesses, no noncompliance.
- **December 11, 2024**: Five-year financial forecast showed:
  - Revenues growing 5.7%/year ($25M) but expenditures growing 7.4%/year ($31M)
  - Enrollment declined 8.7% (2,308 students) over 10 years
  - Unassigned fund balance projected to fall from 9.9% (2024-25) to 0.8% by 2028-29
  - All ESSER funds exhausted ($99.95M total, $94.86M General Fund)

### 2025: Crisis Deepens
- **March 12, 2025**: Staff recommended board consider increasing minimum fund balance from 5% to 10%. Noted "State and Federal funds may be at risk of reduction" and "continuous structural deficit."
- **June 25, 2025**: FY2025-26 budget presented: revenues $555.55M, expenditures $570.43M, deficit $14.88M. Unassigned fund balance 7.3%. Public hearing held -- NO public comments received.
- **October 2025**: K-12 enrollment count showed decline of 792 FTE from adopted budget (AAFTE dropped from 24,222 to 23,430), causing $8.2M revenue reduction. Inclusive Education count showed 413 FTE decline, causing $6.2M revenue reduction.
- **October 17, 2025**: Superintendent's message acknowledged funding gap "grown from $14.9 million to $35.3 million, an increase of $20.4 million."
- **December 3, 2025**: Budget update showed unassigned fund balance at 5.7% -- approaching 5% policy minimum. Operating deficit expanded from $14.9M to $22.8M.

### 2026: Emergency Cuts
- **January 7, 2026**: Board retreat presented need for $35.0M in permanent cuts for 2026-27, plus $9.0M (2027-28) and $10.0M (2028-29). Detailed 18.23 FTE vacant positions and $5.1M MSOC already cut.
- **February 4, 2026**: Revised federal revenue reduction from $8M down to $3.2M, but still needed:
  - 2026-27: $30.5M permanent cuts
  - 2027-28: additional $9.5M
  - 2028-29: additional $9.5M
  - Without cuts: unassigned fund balance would reach NEGATIVE $89.3 million (-15.3%)

### Key Findings
- District consistently OVER-projected enrollment, especially during pandemic years (2020-22 errors of 3.6-4.2%)
- Staff acknowledged: "there were a couple years where we really over-budgeted our enrollment, and that's dangerous"
- Progressive downward revision of kindergarten projections across budget years:
  - FY2022-23 budget projected K FTE at 1,914 for 2022-23, growing to 1,972 by 2025-26
  - FY2023-24 budget revised K FTE down to 1,855 for 2023-24, declining to 1,755 by 2026-27
  - FY2024-25 budget further reduced K FTE to 1,748 for 2024-25, declining to 1,696 by 2027-28
- Total enrollment decline: 1,849 over 10 years, 80% since pandemic

### Projection Methodology
- MGT consulting used weighted average of 4 models: Average Percentage Increase, Cohort Survival, Linear Regression, Student-Age of Population
- Capital facilities planning used Les Kendrick Demographic Study (shifted from "Medium Growth" to "Low Growth" model between 2023 and 2024 CFPs)
- King County live birth data used for kindergarten entry projections
- District tracked OSPI 1251 FTE reports as benchmark but no evidence of formal comparison to independent state projections

## 4. GOVERNANCE ACTIONS (What the Board Did or Didn't Do)

### Formal Actions Taken
1. **Policy 6022 (Fund Balance)**: Adopted minimum 5% unassigned fund balance; triggers restoration plan if below 5%, board notification within 60 days if below 3%
2. **Policy 6000 (Budget Execution)**: Monthly financial reports required; reserve funds require board approval to expend
3. **Policy 1331 (Audit of Expenditures)**: Board approves all bills/payroll at each regular meeting; external audit required
4. **Fiscal Recovery Task Force (Oct 2018)**: Established with monthly monitoring, dashboards, board co-presentations
5. **Superintendent Evaluation (Oct 2022)**: Financial accountability rubric requiring "Distinguished" level to include maintaining adequate reserves and "immediately notifies the board regarding factors that may make the budget unsustainable"
6. **Finance/Audit Committee (Mar 2018)**: President Vengadasalam led discussion to establish committee
7. **Board Governance Retreat (Oct 2025)**: Reviewed Standard 3e on financial sustainability

### Budget Adoption History
- Budgets adopted annually by August 31 per Policy 6000
- Budget work sessions typically held March through August (3-4 sessions per cycle)
- Public hearings held as required by law

### Areas of Concern
- **Fiscal Recovery Task Force**: Established in 2018 but no evidence in documents of ongoing operation or reports after the amended action plan of March 2019
- **Fund Balance Policy**: 5% minimum equals only ~2.5 weeks of operating costs at $47M/month; recommendation to increase to 10% first appeared in March 2025 -- years after structural deficit was identified in 2022
- **Superintendent Evaluation**: Framework requires "immediately notifies" board of unsustainable budget factors, but the scale of deficit deterioration (from $6.5M projected for FY2025-26 in summer 2024 to $22.8M by late 2025) raises questions about timeliness of notification
- **No evidence of independent financial oversight**: SAO audits were clean but covered period ending August 2023 -- before the worst deterioration

## 6. RED FLAGS (Contradictions, Gaps, Suspicious Patterns)

### RED FLAG 1: The $18.8 Million Fund Balance Discrepancy
- At budget adoption (August 21, 2024), Budget Director Tylor told board ending fund balance would be "around $46 million"
- Actual FY2023-24 ending fund balance was $69.79 million -- approximately $18.8 million MORE than budgeted ~$51M
- This variance "was not disclosed" per RAG analysis
- **Question**: How did the district accumulate $18.8M more than projected? Was the board given accurate information during budget adoption?

### RED FLAG 2: Massive Projection Miss for FY2025-26
- Summer 2024 projection: FY2025-26 deficit of $6.50 million
- By late 2025 actual: FY2025-26 deficit of $22.84 million (3.5x the original projection)
- October 2025 enrollment shortfall was 792 FTE (3.3% below adopted budget)
- **Question**: Was the 24,222 AAFTE used for the FY2025-26 budget realistic given years of consistent over-projection? What due diligence was performed?

### RED FLAG 3: Known Structural Deficit Acknowledged but Inadequately Addressed (2022-2024)
- June 2022: Structural deficit explicitly identified; "$30M/year in non-recurring revenue" noted
- August 2022: "Off-Ramping from ESSER" identified as critical 2024 planning issue
- November 2023: $31.5M structural deficit presented to board
- Yet: FY2024-25 budget still assumed only $9.65M deficit; FY2025-26 only $6.50M
- **Question**: If the structural deficit was $31.5M as of November 2023, how were the 2024-25 and 2025-26 budget projections so much lower? What assumptions changed?

### RED FLAG 4: Fiscal Recovery Task Force -- Established Then Disappeared
- Task Force established October 2018 with detailed monthly monitoring requirements
- Action plan amended March 2019
- No evidence of Task Force activity, reports, or dashboards in subsequent documents
- **Question**: What happened to the Fiscal Recovery Task Force? Were its monitoring requirements maintained?

### RED FLAG 5: ESSER Spending Pattern
- Total ESSER: $99.95 million ($94.86M General Fund)
- District acknowledged spending "Roughly $30 million/year in Non-Recurring Revenue leading up to 2024"
- District planned "Off-Ramping from ESSER in 2024-25" but the scale of post-ESSER deficit suggests off-ramping was insufficient
- **Question**: How was ESSER money allocated? Were recurring commitments (positions, programs) funded with one-time money?

### RED FLAG 6: Clean Audit During Growing Crisis
- SAO audit for FY2023 (Sep 2022-Aug 2023): Unmodified opinion, no findings
- During this same period, the structural deficit was $31.5M and enrollment was in multi-year decline
- **Question**: Did the audit scope include review of budget assumptions, enrollment projections, or multi-year financial sustainability? Or was it limited to compliance and financial statement accuracy?

### RED FLAG 7: Superintendent Evaluation vs. Performance
- Evaluation rubric (Oct 2022) requires "Distinguished" to include "immediately notifies the board regarding factors that may make the budget unsustainable"
- The FY2025-26 deficit tripled from projection to reality ($6.5M to $22.8M) within months of budget adoption
- **Question**: Was the superintendent's financial performance evaluated against these specific criteria? What rating was given?

### RED FLAG 8: Classified Staff Bearing Disproportionate Cuts
- 2025-26 cuts: "Certificated salaries were reduced by 0.34%, while classified salaries were reduced by 1.53%"
- Justification: "more classified vacant positions at the time" and "most certificated positions provide direct services to students"
- **Question**: Is this proportionate? What was the composition of the $5.1M MSOC cuts by department?

### RED FLAG 9: Zero Public Comments at $570M Budget Hearing
- June 25, 2025 public hearing on proposed FY2025-26 budget
- "There were no comments from anyone from the public"
- This is a $570M budget with a known $14.88M deficit for a district of 23,000+ students
- **Question**: Was the hearing adequately publicized? Were notices published as required?

### RED FLAG 10: Levy Failure Pattern and Trust Deficit
- Fourth attempt at capital funding levy as of July 2024
- Community member noted district chose levy (simple majority) over bond (60% threshold)
- 2023 bond survey: community concerned about transparency, some perceived "budget surplus"
- Board member (June 2024): "There's a lot of concern and trust in the district, warranted or not, that is what I still to this day believe is causing us to fail these levies"
- EP&O levy expires December 31, 2027 -- during deepest projected deficit period
- **Question**: Has the pattern of failed capital measures contributed to deferred maintenance costs that are now pressuring the operating budget?

### RED FLAG 11: Revenue Growth vs Expenditure Growth Mismatch
- Revenue growing 5.7%/year; expenditures growing 7.4%/year
- This 1.7% annual gap on a ~$500M budget = ~$8.5M/year widening deficit
- **Question**: What specific expenditure categories drove the 7.4% growth? Were collective bargaining agreements, special education costs, or other factors adequately anticipated?

### RED FLAG 12: Special Education Over-Budget Pattern
- Monthly financial reports consistently flagged special education instruction expenditures exceeding budget by $9.6M to $12.79M during FY2024-25
- Inclusive Education October 2025 count showed 413 FTE decline, causing $6.2M revenue reduction
- **Question**: Is the special education funding model (based on enrollment counts) misaligned with actual service delivery costs? Is this a systematic under-budgeting pattern?
